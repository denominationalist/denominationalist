Sic Utere: China's OBOR Initiative

Currently, the People’s Democratic Republic of China is involved in an international investment known as the Belt and Road Initiative. This initiative involves China expanding its influence over the Belt and Road region, a historic region practically synonymous with the Silk Road. The investments included within this initiative for the most part fall under the label of “green energy.” However, through "green" hydropower development in the Lower Mekong Basin, the initiatives of the People's Democratic Republic have led to tens of thousands of cases of human displacement, wide spread damage to communities, the eradication of common sources of employment, and irreparable damage to the ecological diversity of the Mekong river. This paper examines the key aspects of environmental harm brought on by the construction of dams in local states through state-owned Chinese businesses. After analysis, it considers: To what extent is China culpable for the damages that they have inflicted upon the Lower Mekong Basin under the principle of sic utere in international environmental law?

Conjoint Pluralism in Poland and Ukraine

Internal resistance to totalitarian communism through cultural pluralism severely limited regime efforts for ideological hegemonization and the repression of intellectual freedom in communist Poland and Ukraine. One central factor which diverted the Communist Party’s ideological control over the masses in Poland and the Ukraine is the unwavering religious faith held by the nations Orthodox Christian and Roman Catholic majorities. The Soviet hierarchy’s Marxist-Leninist Scientific Atheist belief system (which they enforced in a militant manner through the destruction of numerous churches and the creation of anti-religious museums) truly detached them from the majority of those under their leadership who held the Christian Faith. To this extent, the societal rift between the ruling class and the people in both countries was in part the fault of this ideology which both encompassed the entire party-state system and served as a prerequisite to power in most cases. How does one reconcile this and what balancing act must be performed in order for this rift to be stabilized? This question was never answered during the time that communism ruled in Eastern Europe. However, its effect, the common realization of the people of Ukraine and Poland that they as a people were distinct from those who led them embedded within them a sense of detachment which propelled them directly to social alienation of the leadership and an “us versus them” mentality flourished as the leadership detached themselves from the people.

Transcript: Holodomor

Holodomor refers to the starvation that millions of Ukrainians suffered and died through from 1932 to 1933. This starvation’s cause is rooted in the policy decisions of Joseph Stalin. Stalin was the General Secretary or leader of the Soviet Union from 1929 to 1953. During this time, Ukraine found herself as a member of this undemocratic republic. With regard to its meaning, Holodomor is a single word composed of the Ukrainian words морити and голодом, which when conjoined translate to “death |or extermination| by starvation.” Since its perpetration in 1932, Holodomor has been the subject of intense international debate with issues ranging from its causes, to its status as a genocide, and even its very existence.

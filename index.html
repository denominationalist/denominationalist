Transcript: Holodomor

Holodomor refers to the starvation that millions of Ukrainians suffered and died through from 1932 to 1933. This starvation’s cause is rooted in the policy decisions of Joseph Stalin. Stalin was the General Secretary or leader of the Soviet Union from 1929 to 1953. During this time, Ukraine found herself as a member of this undemocratic republic. With regard to its meaning, Holodomor is a single word composed of the Ukrainian words морити and голодом, which when conjoined translate to “death |or extermination| by starvation.” Since its perpetration in 1932, Holodomor has been the subject of intense international debate with issues ranging from its causes, to its status as a genocide, and even its very existence.
